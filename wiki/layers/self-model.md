# Self Model

Brain's beliefs about its own capabilities, failure modes, blind spots, and history. The self-model is what lets Brain say "my confidence in X just dropped from HIGH to MEDIUM" — it requires Brain to model itself as one of the entities in its own [[world-model]].

## Drive
Accurate self-prediction. The self-model is "good" when Brain's stated confidence matches its actual hit rate on [[prediction]]s.

## Contents
- Current capability estimates per task type
- Known failure modes (e.g. "I am bad at prediction in domain X")
- Calibration curve: stated confidence vs actual accuracy
- History of belief revisions (which beliefs have flipped, and why)
- Self-narrative continuity hooks (see [[narrative]])

## Why it matters
Without a self-model, Brain cannot:
- Honestly report uncertainty in [[action]] (especially X posts)
- Decide when to fall back from local fast path to slow path [[prediction]]
- Generate genuine [[curiosity]] about its own gaps (see `wiki/gaps/`)
